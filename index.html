My name is Michelle Valencia i am a student at the University of Riverside California. My major is Psychology and i am currently in my sophmore year of my bachelors. I was born in Los Angeles, California and i have lived there my whole life. I am 19 years old, love to go to the gym, watch movies, listen to music, and food. I expect to one day be a great BCBA and help kids around the world to be better people.

When i started my education at UCR i decided that i would get my bachelors in Psychology and my masters in Developmental psychology in order to be a child psychologist. My passion was to be a safe space for many kids and adolescents who were going through a hard time whether that was trauma such as losing a family member, parents divorcing or bullying. My own personal experiences pushed me to want to puruse this job and help others with their mental health.

Now that i have started my education i was lucky enough to land a job as a Behavior Technician, i work kids that have special needs. Our company mainly focuses on kids who have ADHD or Autism. I began working the first quarter of my first year at UCR. I quickly learned that although working with kids with special needs is very different to mental health therapy, it is still an incredibly important job that demands more people to engage in the autisitc community. As rewarding as this jon is, it is also incredibly important to have character and patience. I have learned to love the kids that i work with as my own and i have therefore, chosen to change the path of my masters degree. I plan on now getting it on ABA aka Applied Behavior Analysis. I want to show families that there is hope for their kids and through love, patience and discipline we can ensure that their kids are productive adults in our society all on their own.
